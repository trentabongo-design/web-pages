Extranet

Extended Networks — Term 9

What Is an Extranet?

An extranet is a controlled, private network that extends an organisation's intranet to selected external parties — such as business partners, suppliers, clients, or contractors. It sits between the fully private intranet and the fully public internet, giving outsiders limited and controlled access to specific resources.

Think of it as giving certain trusted guests a key to a specific room in your building, without giving them access to the whole place. The rest of the building remains private, but those guests can interact with you in that shared space.

How Extranets Are Used

A manufacturing company might use an extranet to allow its suppliers to view inventory levels and place orders directly. A law firm might use an extranet to share case documents with clients. A university might use an extranet to give alumni access to certain campus resources after they graduate.

In each case, the organisation maintains control over what the external party can see and do. Access is usually protected with secure logins, and the connection is often encrypted to ensure data safety over the public internet.

Extranet vs Intranet vs Internet

The internet is a public network open to everyone. An intranet is a private network accessible only to people within one organisation. An extranet is a middle ground — private like an intranet, but deliberately extended to include select external users. All three use the same core internet technologies, but differ in who is allowed to access what.

Key Facts About Extranets

  • An extranet is a private network that grants controlled access to external users.
  • It extends the functionality of an intranet to trusted partners, suppliers, or clients.
  • Access is typically protected with usernames, passwords, and encrypted connections.
  • Extranets are used heavily in B2B (business-to-business) communication and collaboration.
  • They help streamline supply chains, customer portals, and partner collaboration.
  • An extranet is less open than the internet but less restricted than a full intranet.
