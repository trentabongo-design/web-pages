World Wide Web (WWW)

What is the World Wide Web?

The World Wide Web (WWW) is a system of web pages and documents stored on servers around the world, all connected to each other through hyperlinks. It was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989. You access it using a web browser like Chrome or Firefox. It runs on top of the internet — meaning the internet is the network, and the WWW is the collection of pages and content you can browse through it.
