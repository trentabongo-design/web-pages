Intranet

Private Networks — Term 8

What Is an Intranet?

An intranet is a private network that belongs to an organisation and is only accessible to its authorised members — typically its employees. It uses the same technology as the internet (such as web browsers, HTTP, and TCP/IP), but it is closed off from the public and accessible only from within the organisation or via a secure login.

Large companies, universities, hospitals, and government bodies commonly use intranets to give their staff a centralised place to access internal documents, announcements, tools, and communication platforms without exposing that information to the wider internet.

What Is an Intranet Used For?

Intranets are used for a wide variety of internal purposes. Companies use them to publish internal news and announcements, host HR documents and company policies, provide employee directories, and host collaboration tools. Employees might access internal databases, submit leave requests, or complete training modules — all through the intranet.

Because the intranet is private, organisations can share sensitive business information without it being accessible to competitors or the general public. Security is usually enforced with firewalls, VPNs, and password-protected login systems.

Intranet vs the Internet

The key difference between an intranet and the internet is access. The internet is open to everyone, while an intranet is restricted to a specific group of people. Another way to think about it: the internet is a public road network, while an intranet is like a private road inside a gated estate — the technology is similar, but who can use it is entirely different.

Key Facts About Intranets

  • An intranet is a private network accessible only to authorised users within an organisation.
  • It uses the same underlying technology as the internet (HTTP, TCP/IP, browsers).
  • Firewalls are used to prevent outside access to the intranet.
  • Common uses include internal communications, document storage, and HR systems.
  • Remote employees often access intranets via a VPN (Virtual Private Network).
  • An intranet can improve collaboration, efficiency, and information sharing within a company.
